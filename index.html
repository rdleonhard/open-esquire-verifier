OPEN ESQUIRE

VERIFIER · PUBLIC DOCKET OF ATTORNEY RULINGS
One narrow, durable question per token, answered by a human verifier (a licensed attorney acting personally, not as counsel): does this citation match a case on CourtListener (the Free Law Project's public database)? ✓ YES — a matching citation is on CourtListener · ✗ NO — no matching citation there (unpublished, sealed, or unindexed decisions may not appear) · ⊘ DENIED — the verifier declines to answer (asker refunded). An answer is not a statement that any case is good law, remains valid, or supports any proposition — case law changes constantly and this service does not track it. Built for one job: catching hallucinated citations before they reach a brief.

THE CLERK — FREE AUTOMATED CHECK BETA CHECKING…

Before the human-verified bench, there is the Clerk: a free, automated check any language model can call. It answers the same narrow question — is this citation on CourtListener, and do its parts match? — instantly, with no token and no human, and puts you on notice when a citation resolves but its name, year, or court do not match the reported case. It is not legal advice or a good-law opinion; the attorney-verified answer is the separate paid tier below.
GET /verify?cite=<citation> · see llms.txt. The Clerk is up only when the desk is staffed — check the badge (or /status) before you ping.
